Flood Disaster Information

Learn about flood safety measures and what to do before, during, and after a flood.

Before a Flood

Know Your Risk

Understanding your flood risk is the first step in preparing for potential flooding:

  • Learn about the flood history in your area
  • Check if your property is in a flood-prone zone
  • Stay informed about weather forecasts and flood warnings
  • Sign up for emergency alerts from local authorities

Prepare Your Home

Take these steps to protect your home from flood damage:

  • Elevate electrical systems, water heaters, and appliances above expected flood levels
  • Install check valves in plumbing to prevent floodwater from backing up into drains
  • Seal basement walls with waterproofing compounds
  • Clear gutters and drains regularly
  • Consider installing sump pumps with battery backup

Create an Emergency Plan

Develop a family emergency plan that includes:

  • Evacuation routes and meeting points
  • Emergency contact information
  • Plan for pets and livestock
  • Location of emergency shelters
  • Communication strategy if family members are separated

Prepare an Emergency Kit

Assemble an emergency kit with essential supplies:

  • Water (at least 3 gallons per person)
  • Non-perishable food for at least 3 days
  • Medications and first aid supplies
  • Flashlights and extra batteries
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Personal hygiene items
  • Important documents in waterproof container
  • Cash and emergency contact information

During a Flood

Stay Informed

Keep updated with the latest information:

  • Listen to local radio or TV stations for updates
  • Follow emergency services on social media
  • Pay attention to official warnings and instructions

Evacuation

If authorities order evacuation:

  • Leave immediately - don't wait
  • Follow recommended evacuation routes
  • Do not drive through flooded areas
  • Move to higher ground away from rivers, streams, and drains

If You Cannot Evacuate

If you're trapped by floodwater:

  • Move to the highest level of your home
  • Only go to the roof if necessary and signal for help
  • Do not enter rooms where water covers electrical outlets or cords
  • Avoid contact with floodwater - it may be contaminated

Water Safety

Remember these critical water safety rules:

  • Never walk, swim, or drive through floodwater
  • Just 6 inches of fast-moving water can knock you down
  • Just 12 inches of water can float a vehicle
  • Stay off bridges over fast-moving water

After a Flood

Returning Home

Before returning to your home:

  • Wait for authorities to declare it safe to return
  • Be alert for damaged roads, bridges, and buildings
  • Avoid floodwaters and areas that are still flooded
  • Use caution when entering buildings

Safety Precautions

Take these safety measures when cleaning up:

  • Turn off electricity at the main breaker if water has entered your home
  • Wear protective clothing, including gloves and masks
  • Discard food that has come into contact with floodwater
  • Use bottled water until local water is declared safe
  • Document damage with photos for insurance claims

Health Concerns

Be aware of these health risks after a flood:

  • Contaminated water can cause illness
  • Mold can develop quickly in wet conditions
  • Standing water may become breeding grounds for mosquitoes
  • Stress and anxiety are common after disasters

Recovery Resources

Seek help from these resources:

  • Contact your insurance company to file claims
  • Apply for disaster assistance if available
  • Reach out to local relief organizations
  • Connect with community support groups
